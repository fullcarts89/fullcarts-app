# The Hook System

Based on the four-mistake hook framework (from a pro short-form creator's masterclass), adapted
to FullCarts. The hook is the whole ballgame — 50–60% of drop-off happens in the first 3 seconds.
This is how we write every hook now. The operator applies it; the `interesting` gate enforces it.

## The 4 hook mistakes (and the fix for each)

### 1. Delay — the context of what the video is about comes too late
The viewer can't decide whether to opt in until they know what it's about. Every second without
that context, viewers bounce ("speed to value"). **Fix:** put the topic in the **first sentence,
fewest words possible.** Move the point to the very top — clarity in the first 1–2 seconds.

### 2. Confusion — the viewer isn't sure what the video is about
**Fix:** maximize clarity. The viewer should know *exactly* what they're about to watch. Name the
thing plainly (the product, the number, the claim).

### 3. Irrelevance — the viewer isn't confident it's relevant to *them*
Two fixes, use both:
- **"You / your," never "I / my."** Flip every hook to the viewer. ❌ "I got annoyed my cereal felt
  lighter" → ✅ "If you've felt your cereal box get lighter…". The viewer must see themselves.
- **Frame around a known painpoint (expected value).** Education solves a specific painpoint;
  entertainment solves boredom. Agitate a painpoint they *already have* to tee up the payoff.
  ("A nice-to-have won't hook them; a need-to-have will.") Our painpoint: *getting quietly ripped
  off at the store.*

### 4. Disinterest — clear + relevant, but not *curious* enough to stay
This is the curiosity problem, and it's the one most creators get wrong. **Fix: open a curiosity
loop using CONTRAST.**
- A **curiosity loop**: the viewer sees something → asks a question in their head → gets partial
  context → which spurs the *next* question → and so on. Your hook's job is to open the *first* loop.
- **Contrast** = the distance between what the viewer currently believes (A) and the contrarian /
  alternative thing you're claiming (B). That distance is what creates curiosity. Bigger believable
  distance = stronger pull.
  - **Stated contrast** (blunt, impossible to misread): say both A and B. *"Most people think prices
    just went up. Actually the package got smaller and the price stayed the same."*
  - **Implied contrast** (subtle, savvy): say only B; the baseline A is understood. *"There's a
    second grocery price hike that doesn't show up in any inflation number."*

> **Anti-pattern to KILL:** the vague open-loop hook — *"Guess how much smaller this got,"* *"You
> won't believe this,"* *"This is the craziest thing…"* It gives the viewer **zero context** (mistake
> #1 + #4 at once). It only "works" off the creator's face/text on screen — the spoken words add
> nothing. We do not use these as the hook. (Note: our old first-batch Gatorade hook was exactly this.)

## The FullCarts hook formula

A hook is usually **2–3 lines**, not one punchy line: **clarity first, then contrast.**

```
LINE 1 (clarity, ≤8 words):   name the product + that it shrank   → kills Delay + Confusion
LINE 2 (contrast + you/your): same price / less product, stated or implied → kills Disinterest
(loop):                        tease the "how they hid it" / "the number they don't count"
RELEVANCE:                     second person + the rip-off painpoint → kills Irrelevance
```

Clarity and contrast can collapse into one line when it's tight enough.

## Swipe file (FullCarts niche)

**Stated-contrast templates**
- "You're paying the same price for [product] as [year] — except [X%] of it is gone."
- "[Brand] kept the price identical and quietly took out [amount]. Here's how."
- "The government says groceries rose [X%]. Your receipt says more — here's the number they don't count."

**Implied-contrast templates**
- "There's a second price hike on [product] that doesn't show up in any inflation stat."
- "[Brand] shrank this [X%] and bet you wouldn't weigh it."
- "Your [product] is [X%] smaller than it was — same shelf, same price."

**You/your relevance flips**
- ❌ "I tracked…" → ✅ "If you buy [product], you've been…"
- ❌ "I built a database…" → ✅ "You're not imagining it — and here's the proof."

## Hook checklist (every hook, before it ships)
```
[ ] CLARITY    topic is unmistakable in the first sentence (≤ ~8 words to the point)
[ ] SPEED      no fluff/preamble before the point ("Guys, so…", "Ok this is wild…") — cut it
[ ] YOU        written in second person; viewer sees themselves (not "I/my")
[ ] PAINPOINT  ties to the rip-off feeling (a need-to-have, not a nice-to-have)
[ ] CONTRAST   opens a loop via stated or implied contrast (A vs B distance)
[ ] NOT-VAGUE  not a context-free open loop ("guess how much", "you won't believe")
```
A hook that misses CONTRAST or CLARITY is the most common failure — fix those first.
