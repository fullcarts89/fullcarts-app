Safe zones: overlays sit fully inside the frame — never bleeding off-edge. Keep the −X% badge and key numbers clear of the right ~12% (the like/comment/share rail) and the bottom ~15% (caption + handle), since the app draws its UI there.

Sound palette — “a calm data terminal that slams down a receipt”

Restrained, mechanical/digital, evidence-flavored. Punch comes from two accents (the CAUGHT stamp + the number land), not constant SFX. No glitter/sparkle — it undercuts credibility.

MomentSoundWhy
“Caught:” cold-opena hard stamp / shutter-thunk (one, authoritative) as CAUGHT landsyour sonic logo — “caught red-handed.” Most recognizable cue.
Number reveal / count-upfast odometer/counter roll → soft ding/thunk on stopthe data reveal; mono numbers made audible
Before→after bar shrinksshort descending “deflate” + a tight pop on the −X% badgeyou hear the product get smaller
Data / typing momentssubtle mechanical-keyboard typing under “I do data for a living”reinforces the data-brand; diegetic, not cartoon
Proof / “documented, sourced”receipt-print or a stamp tickthe receipts brand, audible
Cut / transitionclean low whoosh (sparse — every 2–4s cut doesn’t need one)movement without noise
CTAsoft positive UI tap / dinggentle close, not salesy
Underbedlow, tense, minimal drone/beat — quiet under VO (duck −18 dB)keeps authority; voice stays the star

AVOID: glitter/sparkle, riser “vine-booms,” meme SFX, busy stingers. SOURCE: generate custom ones in ElevenLabs SFX (text-to-SFX) for the CAUGHT stamp + counter, then Captions App’s library for the rest. Keep SFX at ~−12 to −18 dB under the voice.
