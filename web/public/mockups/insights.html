The size of shrinkflation, measured.

We cross-reference our database of 2,872 documented events against the BLS R-CPI-SC (the government's official size-change tracker) and FRED's Food-at-Home CPI so you can see whether the brands are shrinking products on schedule with — or ahead of — official inflation.

BLS · Q1 2026
2,341

products downsized last quarter

The Bureau of Labor Statistics' research index R-CPI-SC counts every CPI-tracked item whose package shrank between quarterly surveys. Q1 2026's count is up 14% from Q4 2025; up 38% from Q1 2024. This is the most authoritative measure of US shrinkflation that exists, and we plot it alongside our own catch list below.

Skimpflation: when the recipe quietly changes

Top 8 of 254 flagged products · USDA FoodData Central

It's not just the bag getting smaller. Skimpflation is when brands swap ingredients to lower cost — less meat, more filler, less butter, more palm oil. We cross-reference USDA's quarterly FoodData Central releases to flag nutrition changes that look like ingredient substitution rather than reformulation for taste.
